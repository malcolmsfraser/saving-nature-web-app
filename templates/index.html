Intro

Saving Nature’s mission is to rescue endangered species from extinction and communities from environmental destruction. One of the ways the organization does this is by strategically purchasing “nature corridors” linking previously fragmented habitats. By doing so they restore geographic links between members of endangered species and create opportunities for reproduction.

The number and types of animals that use the nature corridors are used as metrics to measure the efficacy of land purchases. If animals (particularly members of endangered species) are using the corridors, it is indicative that it was an effective purchase. This data is gathered by trail cameras that are strategically placed throughout a nature corridor and automatically turn on when they detect motion. Footage is then reviewed by the saving nature team and used for internal research and evaluation.

This website was designed as an interface for local partners to come and upload video footage retrieved from the trail cameras. Currently, the model used behind the scenes to classify footage is only capable of reviewing footage from Sumatra.

Work

This website acts as the frontend interface to upload Sumatra trail camera footage that is then run through a Machine Learning model to classify the species appearing in the footage. The model was created through a collaborative effort between Saving Nature and students from Duke University’s Master of Interdisciplinary Data Science graduate program.

Manually reviewing video footage is a long and painful process that takes Saving Nature Employee’s a significant amount of time. The object detection model was created to alleviate this burden and reduce the amount of time employees have to spend looking at videos.

The model being used is a Fast R-CNN object detection model. Looking at video footage frame by frame it learns to recognize pixel patterns as whatever animal it is classified as in old footage used as training data. After learning this it is able to take new video footage and predict what animals appear in it with an associated confidence score. If the confidence score reaches a certain threshold, the classification is made.

About

For over a decade, the team at Saving Nature has partnered with local NGO’s to restore ecosystems in Brazil, Colombia, Ecuador, India, and Sumatra. All are important for biodiversity and endemism and all have lost much of their once expansive forests. In preparing for the challenges of the coming decade, we are moving forward with a highly experienced team and a portfolio of new and maturing projects. Also, we are creating a global reference for building effective wildlife corridors.
